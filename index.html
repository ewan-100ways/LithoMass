Corrections
Rose cut style — count the triangular crown facets visible from above.
Heart cleft depth — measure the V-notch at the top as a fraction of total length (point-to-cleft length).
Pear shoulder — where the widest point sits along the length. Find the widest line and see how far below the round end it sits. Higher (closer to the round end) = stubby & heavier; lower (closer to the point) = elongated & lighter.
Girdle thickness — The outer edge or "belt" of the stone. Look at the stone perfectly from the side. 'Standard' is the mathematical baseline. Only select a thicker option if the edge looks visibly prominent or chunky.
Stone bulginess — The shape of the stone's bottom half. Look at the stone from the side. If the lines drop perfectly straight to the bottom point, select 'Standard'. If the bottom looks gently curved or heavily swollen, select a stronger bulge to account for the extra mass.
Old-cut proportions correction — OEC & OMC have a higher crown, larger open culet, and smaller table than modern cuts, adding mass not captured by L×W×D alone
